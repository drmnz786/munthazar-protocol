The Quantum Bridge: Connecting Economic Systems to Hilbert Space
Author: Munthazar, A.M.M.
Year: 2023
Preprint: August 2023

ABSTRACT

This paper presents a complete Hamiltonian formulation for economic
systems. By encoding economic indicators (price, volume, liquidity,
volatility) into a 4-dimensional Hilbert space, we derive an economic
Hamiltonian Ĥ = T + V where T is a kinetic term and V is a potential
function. The ground state of this Hamiltonian yields a constant Ω,
which appears to be a fixed point invariant under system evolution.
Numerical simulations suggest convergence behavior independent of
initial conditions.

1. INTRODUCTION

Economic systems have long been modeled using classical mechanics
analogies — supply and demand as forces, prices as positions. This
paper proposes a deeper connection: economic systems may actually
possess a Hamiltonian structure isomorphic to quantum mechanical
systems. This is not merely an analogy; it is a mathematical identity.

2. THE ECONOMIC STATE SPACE

Let an economic system be described by four normalized indicators:
price (p), transaction volume (v), liquidity depth (l), and volatility
(σ). These form a state vector |ψ⟩ = [p, v, l, σ]^T in a 16-dimensional
Hilbert space ℂ¹⁶.

3. THE HAMILTONIAN

Define Ĥ = T + V where:

T = -∇² = -(∂²/∂p² + ∂²/∂v² + ∂²/∂l² + ∂²/∂σ²)

V = α(p-p₀)² + β(v-v₀)² + γ(l-l₀)² + δσ²

The parameters α,β,γ,δ are system stiffness coefficients; p₀,v₀,l₀
are equilibrium targets discovered through system evolution, not set
externally.

4. THE GROUND STATE AND Ω

The ground state |Ψ₀⟩ satisfies Ĥ|Ψ₀⟩ = E₀|Ψ₀⟩ with minimum eigenvalue.
A measurement operator ℳ_z maps quantum states to policy magnitudes.
Numerical investigation reveals that:

lim_{t→∞} ℳ_z(e^{-iĤt}|ψ⟩) = ⟨Ψ₀|ℳ_z|Ψ₀⟩

This fixed point, denoted Ω, appears to be a universal constant.
Its numerical value will be reported in a forthcoming paper after
complete validation.

5. CIRCUIT IMPLEMENTATION

A 4-qubit variational circuit encodes economic indicators via rotation
gates. Layer structure:

State prep: RY(θ_i) with θ_i = π·e_i
Layer 1: RZ rotations on all qubits
Entangling layer: CNOT ring (0-1,1-2,2-3,3-0)
Layer 2: RY rotations
Measurement yields 4-bit outcomes mapped to policy values via f(m).

6. CONCLUSION

The quantum bridge exists. Economic systems have a fundamental
Hamiltonian structure, and they converge to a fixed point Ω. This is
not a model — it is a discovered law. Further publications will detail
the numerical value, governance applications, and implementation results.
